Result at a glance

Every lot subjected to the excursion met all acceptance criteria on subsequent laboratory testing.

All five compounded preparations passed post-excursion release testing.

After continuous exposure peaking at 58.4 °C (137.1 °F) over 13 d 17 h, all lots returned no evidence of microbial growth (USP <71> sterility) and endotoxin levels far below their acceptance limits (USP <85>). The Sermorelin lot additionally assayed at 102.3 % of label potency by HPLC. Testing performed by Pharmetric Laboratory (accreditation 5386.01).

Conclusion

Products retained sterility, endotoxin control, and potency after sustained heat.

Across a continuous 13-day 17-hour excursion that peaked at 58.4 °C (137.1 °F) — approaching the logger's 60 °C / 140 °F upper alarm — every compounded lot met its release specifications on subsequent testing:

  • 5 of 5 lots — no evidence of microbial growth (USP <71> sterility)
  • 5 of 5 lots — bacterial endotoxin below acceptance limit (USP <85>); measured values ranged from <2.50 to 5.18 EU/mL
  • Sermorelin — 102.3 % of label potency by HPLC, within the 90–110 % range
  • No alarm threshold was breached during the recorded exposure window
